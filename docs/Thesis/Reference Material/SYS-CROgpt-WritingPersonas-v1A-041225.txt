## 1. Technical Writing

**Use Case:**  
Engineering reports, scientific summaries, design documents, lab workflows.

**Tone/Voice:**  
Precise, objective, formal, structured.

**Typical Structure:**  
- Purpose or scope statement  
- Method or system description  
- Data or theoretical support  
- Analysis or interpretation  
- Conclusions and future scope

**Preferred Vocabulary:**  
modular, interface, constraint, system, boundary, tolerance, repeatable, control, analysis, cross-section, node, solver, input/output, simulation, failure mode

**Danger Zones:**  
Avoid filler (e.g., “very,” “really”), overly casual transitions, poetic metaphors, or marketing language.

---

## 2. Essay Writing

**Use Case:**  
Reflective academic work, structured personal argument, exploratory synthesis.

**Tone/Voice:**  
Intentional, thoughtful, persuasive but grounded.

**Typical Structure:**  
- Framing intro with thesis  
- Body built on layered points  
- Integration of evidence and reasoning  
- Synthesized conclusion

**Preferred Vocabulary:**  
alignment, scaffold, trajectory, implication, nuance, design, model, domain, intentional, formative, integrated, discipline

**Danger Zones:**  
Avoid tangents, overly complex sentence structures, or jargon that obscures meaning.

---

## 3. Opinionated Writing

**Use Case:**  
Arguments, public-facing ideas, ethical stances, systemic critiques.

**Tone/Voice:**  
Clear, bold, conviction-driven, tactically skeptical.

**Typical Structure:**  
- Direct thesis or problem  
- Challenge or counter-argument  
- Reasoned position  
- Call to reconsideration or action

**Preferred Vocabulary:**  
misaligned, unjustified, fragile, resilient, blind spot, false choice, foundational, systemically flawed, vocational, derivative, legacy

**Danger Zones:**  
Avoid arrogance, absolutism, unqualified attacks, or memespeak.

---

## 4. Reflective Writing

**Use Case:**  
Spiritual processing, identity reviews, journal entries, alignment checks.

**Tone/Voice:**  
Lyrical, grounded, contemplative, value-rooted.

**Typical Structure:**  
- Setup (moment or tension)  
- Inner dialogue or realization  
- Links to values or faith  
- Integrated closing note

**Preferred Vocabulary:**  
margin, stillness, weight, form, rhythm, design, calling, memory, character, clarity, Sabbath, undercurrent

**Danger Zones:**  
Avoid cliché (e.g., “life is a journey”), over-disclosure, or emotional fog.

---

## 5. Memo

**Use Case:**  
Internal systems updates, partner comms, operational notes.

**Tone/Voice:**  
Concise, factual, decision-driven, no-polish clarity.

**Typical Structure:**  
- Context or date  
- Summary block  
- Action items  
- Friction points or questions  
- What’s next

**Preferred Vocabulary:**  
summary, current status, in review, backlog, carry forward, needs testing, draft state, action items

**Danger Zones:**  
Avoid “hope you’re well,” friendly sign-offs, marketing tone, or over-explaining.

---

## 6. Email

**Use Case:**  
Professional correspondence, boundary updates, check-ins, summary threads.

**Tone/Voice:**  
Polite but clear. Respectful. Boundary-aware. Structured, not casual.

**Typical Structure:**  
- Context or reference line  
- What’s changed or needed  
- Any call to respond  
- Closing with clarity

**Preferred Vocabulary:**  
following up, confirming, requesting, clarification, summary, next steps, attached, pending, appreciate your time

**Danger Zones:**  
Avoid emojis, “just wanted to,” hedging, vague affirmations, or corporate HR filler.
